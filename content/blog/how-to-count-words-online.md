## Tips for Staying Within Word Limits

Hitting a word limit — especially when you're over — can feel frustrating. Here are some practical ways to cut down:

### Cut filler words
Words like "very," "really," "just," "that," and "actually" add length without adding meaning. Try removing them and see if your sentences still make sense.

### Shorten sentences
Long, complex sentences often hide two or three simpler ideas. Break them apart.

### Remove redundancies
"Completely finished," "absolutely certain," and "end result" are all redundant. One word does the job.

### Use active voice
Passive voice uses more words. "The report was written by Sarah" becomes "Sarah wrote the report."
