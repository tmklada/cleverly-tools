## How to Merge PDF Files Online — Free Guide

Need to combine multiple PDF files into one? Whether you're merging contracts, reports, or scanned documents, our free online PDF merger makes it easy — no software, no account, no cost.

## How to Combine PDF Files (3 Steps)

### Step 1: Open the PDF Merger Tool

Go to [cleverly.tools/tools/merge-pdf](/tools/merge-pdf)

### Step 2: Upload Your PDF Files

Click **"Add PDF Files"** and select all the PDFs you want to merge. You can add as many files as you need.

### Step 3: Merge and Download

Click **"Merge PDFs"** and your combined PDF will download instantly.

## Why Merge PDF Files?

- **Work documents:** Combine a cover letter, resume, and portfolio into one file
- **Contracts:** Merge multiple agreement pages into a single document
- **Reports:** Combine monthly reports into one annual document
- **Scans:** Merge multiple scanned pages into one PDF

## Can I Choose the Order of Pages?

Yes! You can reorder the PDF files before merging — just drag them into the order you want.

## Is My Data Safe?

All PDF processing happens **directly in your browser**. Your files are never uploaded to our servers. This means:
- ✅ Complete privacy — your documents stay on your device
- ✅ Works offline (once the page loads)
- ✅ No file size limits imposed by server bandwidth

## Alternative: Merge PDFs on iPhone

Our tool works in Safari on iPhone:
1. Go to cleverly.tools/tools/merge-pdf
2. Tap "Add PDF Files" → choose from Files app
3. Tap "Merge PDFs"
4. The merged PDF downloads to your Files → Downloads folder

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many PDFs can I merge at once?**
As many as you want. The only limit is your device's memory.

**Q: Will merging PDFs reduce quality?**
No. The PDF content is preserved exactly — images, text, fonts, and formatting remain intact.

**Q: Can I merge password-protected PDFs?**
No. Password-protected PDFs need to be unlocked first.

**Q: What's the maximum file size?**
Since processing happens in your browser, very large files (500MB+) may be slow depending on your device.
