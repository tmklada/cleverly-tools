## JPG vs. PNG vs. WebP — Which Format Should You Use?

This is the most important thing to understand about image formats. Each one has a specific purpose:

| Format | Best For | Transparency | File Size |
|--------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **JPG** | Photos, social media, emails | No | Small |
| **PNG** | Logos, screenshots, graphics | Yes | Medium-Large |
| **WebP** | Websites, web images | Yes | Very Small |
| **GIF** | Simple animations | Yes (limited) | Medium |
| **BMP** | Windows system images | No | Very Large |

### When to Use JPG
JPG (also called JPEG) is the go-to format for photographs. It compresses images efficiently, keeping file sizes small while maintaining good visual quality. Use JPG when:
- Uploading photos to social media
- Sending images by email
- Displaying photos on a website
- File size is more important than perfect quality

### When to Use PNG
PNG preserves every pixel perfectly and supports transparent backgrounds. Use PNG when:
- Your image has a logo or text on a transparent background
- You need pixel-perfect quality (screenshots, graphics)
- You plan to edit the image further
- Quality must not be compromised at all

### When to Use WebP
WebP is a modern format developed by Google. It's smaller than both JPG and PNG while keeping excellent quality — ideal for websites. Use WebP when:
- Optimizing images for web pages
- You need the best balance of quality and file size
- Your CMS or website platform supports it

## Common Conversion Scenarios

**"I have a PNG logo but need it as JPG for an email campaign"**
Convert PNG → JPG. Note: JPG doesn't support transparent backgrounds, so any transparency will become white.

**"I have a JPG photo but need to add it to a design with a transparent background"**
Convert JPG → PNG, then remove the background in a design tool.

**"I need to upload product images to my website without slowing it down"**
Convert JPG or PNG → WebP. WebP files are 25–35% smaller, which makes your pages load faster.

**"I got a WebP file but my email client won't display it"**
Convert WebP → JPG. Most email clients support JPG universally.

## Does Converting Images Reduce Quality?

It depends on the conversion direction:

- **JPG → PNG:** No quality loss. PNG is lossless.
- **PNG → JPG:** Slight quality loss (JPG is lossy). Transparent areas become white.
- **Any format → WebP:** Minimal quality loss at high quality settings.
- **JPG → JPG (re-saving):** Compresses again, small quality loss. Avoid unnecessary re-saves.

For most practical purposes, the quality difference between formats is invisible to the naked eye — especially at normal viewing sizes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I convert a HEIC photo (from iPhone) to JPG?**
HEIC is the default iPhone photo format. If the converter supports HEIC uploads, it will convert to JPG. If not, change your iPhone camera settings to "Most Compatible" (in Settings → Camera → Formats) to shoot in JPG from the start.

**Q: Does converting PNG to JPG remove the transparent background?**
Yes. JPG doesn't support transparency, so transparent areas are filled with white by default. If you need to keep transparency, convert to PNG or WebP instead.

**Q: Is there a quality difference I can see between JPG and PNG?**
For photographs, JPG quality at 80–90% is virtually indistinguishable from PNG. For logos, text, and graphics with sharp edges, PNG looks noticeably crisper.
