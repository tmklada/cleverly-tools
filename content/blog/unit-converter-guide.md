## Why Unit Conversions Are So Common

The world uses two major systems of measurement:

- **The Metric System (SI)** — used by most of the world, based on multiples of 10. Meters, kilograms, liters.
- **The Imperial System** — used primarily in the United States. Feet, pounds, gallons.

The problem is that these systems don't overlap cleanly, and the math isn't always intuitive. One mile is 1.60934 kilometers. One pound is 453.592 grams. One US gallon is 3.78541 liters. Remembering these conversions — or calculating them — is exactly the kind of task a tool should handle for you.

## Tips for Accurate Unit Conversion

- **Double-check the category.** A fluid ounce (volume) is different from an ounce (weight). Make sure you're in the right conversion category.
- **Watch for US vs. UK differences.** US and UK gallons, pints, and tons are different. Our converter specifies which system is being used.
- **Use enough decimal places.** For critical applications like engineering or medication dosing, precision matters. Don't round off too early.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between weight and mass?**
Mass is the amount of matter in an object (measured in grams or kilograms). Weight is the force gravity exerts on that mass (measured in newtons). In everyday usage, people use "weight" to mean mass — and our converter follows everyday convention.
